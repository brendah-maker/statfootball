Arsenal Crowned 2025/26 Premier League Champions as Bournemouth Stun Man City

SF

StatFootball Analytics Desk

May 19, 2026 • 8 Min Read

The 22-year wait is over. Arsenal have officially clinched the Premier League title following a historic night at the Vitality Stadium where AFC Bournemouth defeated Manchester City 1-0.

In a result that has sent shockwaves through the footballing world, **Mikel Arteta’s Arsenal** are the champions of England. The title race was effectively settled not in North London, but on the South Coast, as Andoni Iraola’s tactical masterclass completely dismantled a leggy and uninspired Manchester City side.

Promoted Story

Junior Kroupi: The Boy Who Ended the Race

The decisive blow was struck in the 40th minute by the teenage sensation **Junior Kroupi**. The 19-year-old, who has been the breakout star of the 2025/26 campaign, latched onto a defense-splitting pass from Adrien Truffert. With the composure of a veteran, Kroupi shimmyed past Mateo Kovacic and fired a low, precision strike into the bottom corner of Gianluigi Donnarumma’s net.

Manchester City, chasing a record-extending title run, looked a shadow of their usual selves. With reports circulating that this marks the final week of **Pep Guardiola’s departure**, the Citizens lacked the clinical edge required to break down a Bournemouth defense that has now gone 17 games unbeaten.

The Gunners' Glory: Standings Confirmed

With this result, Arsenal sit five points clear at the top of the **Premier League standings** with just one game remaining. For Mikel Arteta, this represents the culmination of a six-year project. Since the "Invincibles" era of 2004, the Gunners have faced heartbreak, but the 2025/26 season will go down as the year they finally cracked the code.

City’s loss ends their dominance and marks a significant shift in the English football power dynamic. Erling Haaland was kept quiet throughout the 90 minutes by James Hill, a testament to the growth of this Bournemouth squad under Iraola.
