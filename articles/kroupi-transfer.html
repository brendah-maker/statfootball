Man City Target £100m Move for Eli Junior Kroupi: The French Sensation Ending Title Races

The summer transfer window has its first major saga. Manchester City have identified 19-year-old AFC Bournemouth striker Eli Junior Kroupi as their primary attacking target, with the South Coast club reportedly holding out for a record-breaking fee in the region of £80 million to £100 million.

The Valuation

Bournemouth paid just £11M to Lorient in early 2025. A £100M sale would represent a staggering 800% profit in just over a year.

A New Transfer Tussle

According to reports, Kroupi’s deal at the Vitality Stadium contains no release clause, putting Bournemouth in a formidable negotiating position. Manchester City director Hugo Viana has reportedly already met with the player’s representatives to discuss a move to the Etihad Stadium.

However, the Citizens face intense competition. Kroupi’s meteoric rise has caught the attention of domestic rivals Arsenal and Chelsea, while European heavyweights Barcelona and Paris Saint-Germain are also monitoring the situation closely.

Historical Impact: The Robbie Keane Comparison

The hype surrounding the French teenager is backed by historic data. Kroupi recently became the first teenager since Robbie Keane in the 1999–2000 season to score at least 11 goals in a debut Premier League campaign. His clinical finishing and tactical intelligence have led scouts to compare him to a young Thierry Henry.

The Goal That Crowned Arsenal

Perhaps the most compelling reason for City’s interest is Kroupi’s direct impact on the 2025/26 title race. In the final weeks of the season, Kroupi scored a stunning winner against Manchester City themselves. That result effectively ended Pep Guardiola’s hopes of another title and handed the championship to Arsenal—a bitter irony that City now hopes to remedy by bringing the striker to Manchester.

Will Kroupi Move?

Sources close to the player indicate that Kroupi is eager to "test himself at the highest level," making a summer departure highly likely if Bournemouth's valuation is met.
