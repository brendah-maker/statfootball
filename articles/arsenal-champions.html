Arsenal Crowned 2025/26 Premier League Champions as Bournemouth Stun Man City

SF

Ed Aarons

May 19, 2026 • 6 Min Read

The 22-year wait is finally over. Arsenal are Premier League champions for the first time since Arsène Wenger’s Invincibles in 2004 after Manchester City were held to a 1-1 draw at Bournemouth.

Mikel Arteta’s squad gathered at the London Colney training ground to watch the drama unfold on the South Coast. As the final whistle blew at the Vitality Stadium, scenes of absolute jubilation erupted. Declan Rice was the first to break the digital silence, posting an Instagram photo alongside Kai Havertz, Bukayo Saka, and Eberechi Eze with the caption: “I told you all... it’s done.”

Historical Milestone

At 44, Mikel Arteta becomes the second-youngest manager to win the Premier League, trailing only José Mourinho's 2005 record.

Wenger's Blessing

The club marked the achievement with a poignant video introduced by the legendary Arsène Wenger. Raising a glass of red wine, the man who last delivered the title to Highbury told the new generation: “Champions go on when others stop. Now go on and enjoy every moment.”

It marks a historic transformation under Arteta, who took over in 2019. After three consecutive seasons finishing as runners-up, the Gunners finally emerged strongest from what effectively became a five-game playoff at the end of the season. While City blinked first in a draw against Everton, Arsenal recorded four successive wins without conceding a single goal.

By The Numbers: A Defensive Masterclass

The 14th English title for the club was built on a historic defensive foundation. David Raya has officially claimed his third successive Golden Glove after Arsenal kept a league-high 19 clean sheets. Having won 25 of their 37 games so far, the Gunners' consistency proved too much for a City side that led the league for 200 days before faltering in the final month.

The celebrations have already spilled out of the Hertfordshire training base and onto the streets of North London. Thousands of fans are currently marching towards the Emirates Stadium, where club legend Ian Wright was seen joining the festivities.

The Budapest Dream

Arsenal will officially receive the Premier League trophy this Sunday at Selhurst Park following their final game against Crystal Palace. However, the season doesn't end there. On May 30, Arteta’s men head to Budapest to face Paris Saint-Germain in the Champions League final, looking to secure a historic Double.
