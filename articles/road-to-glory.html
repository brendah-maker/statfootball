Road to Glory: 10 Defining Moments of Arsenal's 2025/26 Title Triumph

Every championship-winning season is defined by a handful of turning points—the moments where pressure meets preparation. As Arsenal celebrates its 14th Premier League title and the end of a 22-year drought, we look back at the tactical shifts, last-gasp goals, and heroic saves that brought the trophy back to North London.

01. Martinelli Stuns Manchester City

In September, the title race faced an early litmus test. Trailing Manchester City to an Erling Haaland strike, Mikel Arteta turned to the bench. In the 84th minute, Gabriel Martinelli entered the fray. Deep into stoppage time, an Eberechi Eze delivery found the Brazilian, who lobbed Gianluigi Donnarumma to snatch a vital point. This moment signaled that Arsenal was finally ready to stand toe-to-toe with the champions.

02. The St. James’ Park Turnaround

Winning at Newcastle United had become a psychological hurdle for the Gunners. When Nick Woltemade put the Magpies ahead, it felt like history repeating itself. However, a Mikel Merino header followed by a dramatic 90th-minute Gabriel Magalhães winner sparked pandemonium in the away end, ending the Newcastle hoodoo for good.

03. Eze’s Scissor-Kick Debut

Eberechi Eze announced himself as a North London icon in October. In a tight London derby against Crystal Palace, Eze met a cleared free-kick with a sensational scissor kick that found the bottom corner. It was a goal of pure technical brilliance that kept Arsenal at the summit of the table.

04. Stoppage Time Chaos vs Wolves

Following a loss to Aston Villa, Arsenal faced a stubborn Wolves side. When the visitors equalized in the final minute, the title race looked to be slipping. Yet, under intense pressure, a Bukayo Saka cross forced a 94th-minute own goal, securing a dramatic 2-1 victory and launching a vital five-match winning streak.

05. David Raya’s Save of the Season

David Raya’s Golden Glove campaign was anchored by his performance against Brighton. With the Seagulls trailing 2-1 and pressing hard, Raya produced an acrobatic, fingertip save to deny Yankuba Minteh. The save was later voted Premier League Save of the Month and preserved three crucial points.

06. Mosquera’s Heroic Recovery

Defense wins titles, and Cristhian Mosquera proved it at Brentford in February. With the scores level and Brentford’s Igor Thiago clean through on goal, Mosquera produced a lung-busting recovery tackle in the 87th minute. It was a "goal-saving" intervention that epitomized Arsenal’s new-found grit.

07. Denying Chelsea at the Death

The London Derby in March was a test of nerves. After Jurrien Timber and William Saliba put Arsenal ahead, Chelsea launched a late assault. Raya was forced into a world-class double save, first clawing away a Garnacho cross and then stopping a João Pedro overhead kick to secure the win.

08. Gyökeres and the Dowman Impact

As the spring pressure mounted, Viktor Gyökeres proved to be the ultimate super-sub. Against Everton, the Swede prodded home a late winner after a cross from teenage sensation Max Dowman. This game highlighted the incredible depth of Arteta’s 2025/26 squad.

09. Eze Sinks the Magpies (Again)

Following a difficult exit from the FA Cup and a loss to Manchester City, Arsenal’s title hopes were on a knife-edge in April. Eberechi Eze stepped up once again, curling an early strike into the top corner against Newcastle. The 1-0 victory restored momentum just as the finish line came into view.

10. Trossard Seals the Deal at West Ham

The final hurdle was a visit to the London Stadium. After Raya kept the game level with a stunning one-on-one save against Matheus Fernandes, Leandro Trossard clinical finish five minutes later confirmed the 1-0 win. It was the result that effectively brought the Premier League trophy back to the Emirates for the first time in 22 years.
